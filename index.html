Kanda Poha (Savory Flattened Rice)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 15 mins

\

Serves: 2

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1.5 cups thick Poha (flattened rice)
    • \
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
    • \
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
    • \
  • A few curry leaves
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
    • \
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
    • \
  • 1 tbsp oil
    • \
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Wash poha gently under running water in a colander until soft, drain and set aside.
    2. \
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add curry leaves and green chilies.
    3. \
  3. Add chopped onions and saut\'e9 until translucent.
    4. \
  4. Stir in turmeric powder and roasted peanuts.
    5. \
  5. Add the drained poha and salt. Mix gently until well combined.
    6. \
  6. Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes on low heat.
    7. \
  7. Squeeze lemon juice over the poha and garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot.
    8. \
\

Tips or Variations: Add finely chopped potatoes or peas for extra flavor. A pinch of sugar can balance the taste.

\

Personal Note: This reminds me of Sunday mornings at Grandma's house, a perfect start to the day!

Masala Omlette

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 10 mins

\

Serves: 1

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 2 large eggs
    • \
  • 1/4 small onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
    • \
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh coriander
    • \
  • Pinch of turmeric powder
    • \
  • Pinch of red chili powder (optional)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • 1 tbsp oil or butter
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. In a bowl, crack the eggs. Add chopped onion, green chili, coriander, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt.
    2. \
  2. Whisk everything together until well combined and slightly frothy.
    3. \
  3. Heat oil or butter in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
    4. \
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the hot pan, spreading it evenly.
    5. \
  5. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the edges set and the bottom is golden brown.
    6. \
  6. Carefully flip the omlette and cook for another 1-2 minutes until cooked through.
    7. \
  7. Fold the omlette in half and slide onto a plate.
    8. \
\

Tips or Variations: Add a pinch of garam masala for extra flavor. You can also add grated cheese or finely chopped bell peppers.

\

Personal Note: A quick and protein-packed breakfast, perfect for busy mornings!

Upma (Savory Semolina Porridge)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 20 mins

\

Serves: 2

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Rava (semolina/sooji)
    • \
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
    • \
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • \
  • 1 tsp Urad Dal (split black lentils)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Chana Dal (split chickpeas)
    • \
  • A few curry leaves
    • \
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, beans - optional)
    • \
  • 2.5 cups hot water
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
    • \
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Heat a dry pan and roast the rava on medium-low heat until fragrant and light golden (about 5-7 minutes). Transfer to a plate and set aside.
    2. \
  2. In the same pan, heat oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds, Urad Dal, and Chana Dal. Saut\'e9 until dals turn light golden.
    3. \
  3. Add curry leaves and green chili. Saut\'e9 for a few seconds.
    4. \
  4. Add chopped onion and mixed vegetables (if using). Saut\'e9 until onions are translucent and vegetables are slightly tender.
    5. \
  5. Pour in the hot water and add salt. Bring to a rolling boil.
    6. \
  6. Reduce heat to low. Gradually add the roasted rava in a thin stream, stirring continuously with one hand to prevent lumps.
    7. \
  7. Mix well, cover, and cook on low heat for 5-7 minutes, or until all water is absorbed and rava is cooked.
    8. \
  8. Turn off heat, add lemon juice, and fluff with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot.
    9. \
\

Tips or Variations: Roasting rava properly is key to non-sticky upma. You can add roasted peanuts or cashews for crunch.

\

Personal Note: A wholesome and comforting South Indian breakfast that's surprisingly easy to make!

Sabudana Khichdi (Tapioca Pearl Pilaf)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 25 mins (plus overnight soaking)

\

Serves: 2-3

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Sabudana (tapioca pearls), medium-sized
    • \
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely crushed
    • \
  • 1 medium potato, boiled, peeled, and diced
    • \
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • A few curry leaves
    • \
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • \
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
    • \
  • Salt to taste (preferably rock salt for fasting)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional, balances flavors)
    • \
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
    • \
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Soak Sabudana: Rinse sabudana thoroughly under running water 2-3 times until the water runs clear. Soak in just enough water to cover the pearls (about 1 inch above the sabudana) overnight or for 4-6 hours until soft and easily mashed between fingers. Drain any excess water completely.
    2. \
  2. In a bowl, combine the soaked and drained sabudana with coarsely crushed peanuts, salt, and sugar (if using). Mix gently.
    3. \
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a non-stick pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    4. \
  4. Add green chilies and curry leaves. Saut\'e9 for a few seconds.
    5. \
  5. Add the diced boiled potatoes and saut\'e9 for 2-3 minutes until lightly golden.
    6. \
  6. Add the sabudana-peanut mixture to the pan. Mix gently but thoroughly.
    7. \
  7. Cook on low to medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sabudana pearls turn translucent. Do not overcook, as they can become sticky or hard.
    8. \
  8. Turn off the heat. Squeeze in the lemon juice and mix well.
    9. \
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
    10. \
\

Tips or Variations: Proper soaking is key to non-sticky khichdi. You can add a pinch of ginger paste to the tempering for extra flavor. Serve with sweet curd or a side of chutney.

\

Personal Note: A beloved dish, especially during fasting, but equally delicious for a light and wholesome breakfast any day!

Idli Vada (Steamed Rice Cakes & Savory Lentil Fritters)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 45 mins (plus 6-8 hrs soaking & 8-12 hrs fermentation)

\

Serves: 4-6

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients for Idli:
\
    \
  • 2 cups Idli Rice (parboiled rice)
    • \
  • 1 cup Urad Dal (split black gram, skinless)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Fenugreek seeds (Methi Dana)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Water for soaking and grinding
    • \
\
Ingredients for Medu Vada:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Urad Dal (split black gram, skinless)
    • \
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped or grated
    • \
  • A few curry leaves, chopped
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
    • \
  • Pinch of Asafoetida (Hing)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Oil for deep frying
    • \
\
Method for Idli:
\
    \
  1. Rinse Idli rice and Urad Dal (with fenugreek seeds) separately until water runs clear. Soak rice for 6-8 hours and Urad Dal for 3-4 hours.
    2. \
  2. Drain the soaked Urad Dal and grind it into a very smooth, fluffy batter using minimal chilled water. The batter should be light and airy.
    3. \
  3. Drain the soaked rice and grind it into a slightly coarse paste (like fine semolina).
    4. \
  4. Combine both batters in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well with your hands for 2-3 minutes. This helps in fermentation.
    5. \
  5. Cover and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours, or until it doubles in volume and becomes light and airy.
    6. \
  6. Once fermented, gently mix the batter. Grease idli molds with oil, pour batter into each mold.
    7. \
  7. Steam in an idli steamer for 10-15 minutes on medium-high heat, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Let rest for 2 minutes before demolding.
    8. \
\
Method for Medu Vada:
\
    \
  1. Rinse Urad Dal thoroughly and soak for 2-3 hours. Drain completely.
    2. \
  2. Grind the soaked Urad Dal into a very thick, smooth, and fluffy batter using minimal (ice-cold) water. It should be thick enough to hold its shape.
    3. \
  3. Transfer the batter to a bowl. Add chopped green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and salt. Mix well.
    4. \
  4. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadhai or deep pan over medium heat.
    5. \
  5. Wet your hands with water. Take a small portion of the batter, flatten it slightly, and make a hole in the center with your thumb.
    6. \
  6. Gently slide the shaped vada into the hot oil. Fry in batches, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy on all sides.
    7. \
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
    8. \
\

Tips or Variations: For idlis, ensure proper fermentation; for vadas, the batter consistency is key \'96 it should be thick and fluffy. Serve both hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

\

Personal Note: The quintessential South Indian breakfast combo! Perfect for a hearty and flavorful start to the day!

Dosa (Savory Indian Crepes)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 30 mins (plus 6-8 hrs soaking & 8-12 hrs fermentation)

\

Serves: 4-6 (makes 10-12 dosas)

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 2 cups Dosa Rice (or raw rice)
    • \
  • 1/2 cup Urad Dal (split black gram, skinless)
    • \
  • 1/4 tsp Fenugreek seeds (Methi Dana)
    • \
  • 1/4 cup Poha (flattened rice, thick variety)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Water for soaking and grinding
    • \
  • Oil or ghee for cooking dosas
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Rinse rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately until water runs clear. Soak rice and urad dal with fenugreek for 6-8 hours. Soak poha for 30 minutes just before grinding.
    2. \
  2. Drain the soaked urad dal and fenugreek. Grind into a very smooth, fluffy batter using minimal chilled water.
    3. \
  3. Drain the soaked rice and poha. Grind into a smooth or slightly coarse batter (finer than idli batter) using minimal chilled water.
    4. \
  4. Combine both batters in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well with your hands for 2-3 minutes. This helps in fermentation.
    5. \
  5. Cover and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours, or until it doubles in volume and becomes light and airy.
    6. \
  6. Once fermented, gently mix the batter. Add a little water if needed to adjust consistency (should be thinner than idli batter, but not too watery).
    7. \
  7. Heat a non-stick tawa or cast-iron griddle over medium-high heat. Sprinkle a few drops of water; if it sizzles and evaporates quickly, the tawa is ready. Wipe with an onion half or damp cloth.
    8. \
  8. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the tawa. Quickly spread it outwards in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
    9. \
  9. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and over the dosa. Cook until the edges turn golden brown and crisp, and the center is cooked through.
    10. \
  10. Fold the dosa in half or roll it up. Serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.
    11. \
\

Tips or Variations: The key to crispy dosas is a well-fermented batter and a hot tawa. For Masala Dosa, spread a potato filling inside before folding. You can also add finely chopped onions or green chilies to the batter.

\

Personal Note: A versatile and delicious staple in South Indian cuisine, perfect for breakfast or a light meal!

Dhokla (Savory Steamed Chickpea Flour Cake)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 25 mins

\

Serves: 4

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients for Dhokla:
\
    \
  • 1.5 cups Besan (gram flour)
    • \
  • 2 tbsp fine Semolina (Sooji/Rava)
    • \
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Green Chili paste (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Sugar
    • \
  • 1/4 tsp Turmeric powder
    • \
  • Pinch of Asafoetida (Hing)
    • \
  • 1 tbsp Lemon juice
    • \
  • 1 tbsp Oil
    • \
  • 1 cup Water (or as needed)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Eno fruit salt (plain, unflavored)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
\
Ingredients for Tempering:
\
    \
  • 2 tbsp Oil
    • \
  • 1 tsp Mustard seeds
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin seeds (optional)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Sesame seeds (Til)
    • \
  • A few Curry leaves
    • \
  • 2-3 Green chilies, slit lengthwise
    • \
  • 1/4 cup Water
    • \
  • 1 tsp Sugar
    • \
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
    • \
  • 1 tsp Lemon juice
    • \
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
    • \
  • Grated coconut for garnish (optional)
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Prepare Dhokla Batter: In a large mixing bowl, sieve besan and semolina. Add ginger-green chili paste, sugar, turmeric powder, asafoetida, lemon juice, oil, and salt.
    2. \
  2. Gradually add water, whisking continuously to form a smooth, lump-free batter of flowing consistency (not too thick, not too thin). Whisk well for 2-3 minutes to aerate the batter.
    3. \
  3. Grease a steaming pan or cake tin with oil.
    4. \
  4. Steam Dhokla: Just before steaming, add Eno fruit salt to the batter. Pour 1 tablespoon of water over the Eno to activate it. Gently mix the batter until it becomes frothy and light. Do not overmix.
    5. \
  5. Immediately pour the frothy batter into the greased pan.
    6. \
  6. Heat water in a steamer or pressure cooker (without whistle). Place the dhokla pan inside. Steam on medium-high heat for 15-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
    7. \
  7. Remove from steamer and let it cool slightly. Cut into squares.
    8. \
  8. Prepare Tempering: In a small pan, heat oil. Add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add cumin seeds (if using), sesame seeds, curry leaves, and slit green chilies. Saut\'e9 for a few seconds.
    9. \
  9. Add water, sugar, and salt to the tempering. Bring to a boil until sugar dissolves. Turn off heat and add lemon juice.
    10. \
  10. Pour the warm tempering evenly over the cut dhokla pieces.
    11. \
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and grated coconut (if using). Serve warm with green chutney or tamarind chutney.
    12. \
\

Tips or Variations: Ensure the batter consistency is right \'96 it should be thick yet pourable. Do not add Eno until just before steaming. For a traditional touch, use citric acid instead of lemon juice. Avoid adding too much turmeric as it can react with Eno and turn the dhokla reddish.

\

Personal Note: A light, spongy, and tangy snack that's a staple in Gujarati households and a healthy breakfast option!

Cheela (Savory Pancakes)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 20 mins

\

Serves: 2-3

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Besan (gram flour)
    • \
  • 1/4 cup Fine Semolina (Sooji/Rava - optional, for crispiness)
    • \
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped mixed vegetables (onion, tomato, capsicum, grated carrot - optional)
    • \
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Green Chili paste
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
    • \
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin powder
    • \
  • Pinch of Asafoetida (Hing)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • 1.5 cups Water (or as needed for batter consistency)
    • \
  • Oil or Ghee for cooking
    • \
  • Fresh coriander, chopped for garnish
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine besan, semolina (if using), turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, asafoetida, and salt.
    2. \
  2. Gradually add water, whisking continuously to form a smooth, lump-free batter of pouring consistency (like pancake batter). Ensure there are no lumps.
    3. \
  3. Stir in the finely chopped mixed vegetables (if using), ginger-green chili paste, and chopped fresh coriander. Mix well.
    4. \
  4. Cover the batter and let it rest for 10-15 minutes. This allows the semolina to swell (if used).
    5. \
  5. Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
    6. \
  6. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the hot tawa and spread it evenly in a circular motion to form a thin pancake.
    7. \
  7. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and over the cheela.
    8. \
  8. Cook until the edges start to crisp and the top looks cooked (about 2-3 minutes).
    9. \
  9. Flip the cheela and cook on the other side until golden brown spots appear and it's cooked through (about 1-2 minutes).
    10. \
  10. Remove from tawa and serve hot. Repeat for remaining batter.
    11. \
\

Tips or Variations: For crispier cheelas, add more semolina or rice flour. You can add grated paneer to the batter for a protein-rich version. Serve with green chutney, tomato ketchup, or yogurt.

\

Personal Note: A versatile and healthy breakfast or snack, perfect for a quick and nutritious meal!

Pancakes (Fluffy Breakfast Delights)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 20 mins

\

Serves: 2-3 (makes 6-8 pancakes)

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup All-purpose flour (Maida)
    • \
  • 2 tbsp Sugar
    • \
  • 1 tsp Baking powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Baking soda
    • \
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
    • \
  • 1 large Egg
    • \
  • 1 cup Milk
    • \
  • 2 tbsp Melted butter or Oil, plus more for cooking
    • \
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract (optional)
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
    2. \
  2. In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients: egg, milk, melted butter/oil, and vanilla extract (if using).
    3. \
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Whisk gently until just combined. A few lumps are fine; do not overmix, as this will result in tough pancakes.
    4. \
  4. Heat a non-stick griddle or pan over medium-low heat. Lightly grease it with butter or oil.
    5. \
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the hot griddle.
    6. \
  6. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges look set.
    7. \
  7. Flip the pancake and cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.
    8. \
  8. Remove from griddle and serve hot. Repeat for remaining batter.
    9. \
\

Tips or Variations: For extra fluffy pancakes, let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes. Serve with maple syrup, honey, fresh fruits, whipped cream, or chocolate chips. You can add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg to the dry ingredients for a warm flavor.

\

Personal Note: A classic breakfast treat that's always a hit, especially on lazy weekend mornings!

Appam (Lacy Rice Pancakes)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 30 mins (plus soaking & fermentation)

\

Serves: 4-5 (makes 10-12 appams)

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1.5 cups Raw Rice (Sona Masuri or Idli Rice)
    • \
  • 1/2 cup Cooked Rice
    • \
  • 1/4 cup Grated Coconut (fresh or frozen)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Dry Yeast
    • \
  • 1 tsp Sugar
    • \
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
    • \
  • Water, as needed for grinding and batter consistency
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Rinse raw rice thoroughly and soak in water for 4-5 hours.
    2. \
  2. In a blender, combine soaked raw rice (drained), cooked rice, grated coconut, and a little water. Grind to a very smooth batter. The batter should be thick but pourable.
    3. \
  3. In a small bowl, dissolve dry yeast and sugar in 2 tbsp warm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
    4. \
  4. Add the activated yeast mixture and salt to the rice batter. Mix well.
    5. \
  5. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours, or until it doubles in volume and becomes light and airy.
    6. \
  6. Once fermented, gently stir the batter. If it's too thick, add a little water to adjust to a pouring consistency.
    7. \
  7. Heat an appam pan (appachatti) over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil.
    8. \
  8. Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the appam pan. Immediately swirl the pan to spread the batter thinly around the edges, creating a lacy pattern. The center will be thicker.
    9. \
  9. Cover the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the edges are golden and crispy, and the center is soft and cooked through.
    10. \
  10. Remove the appam from the pan. Repeat for remaining batter.
    11. \
\

Tips or Variations: Proper fermentation is key for soft and lacy appams. Adjust sugar for desired sweetness. Serve hot with vegetable stew, coconut milk, or egg curry. For a quicker version, you can use baking soda instead of yeast, but the texture might vary.

\

Personal Note: These delicate and fluffy appams with crispy edges are a South Indian delight, perfect for a special breakfast!

Neer Dosa (Thin Rice Crepes)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 25 mins (plus soaking)

\

Serves: 3-4 (makes 10-12 dosas)

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Raw Rice (Sona Masuri or Dosa Rice)
    • \
  • 1/4 cup Grated Coconut (fresh or frozen, optional)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • 2-3 cups Water (or as needed for thin batter)
    • \
  • Oil for cooking dosas
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Rinse raw rice thoroughly and soak in water for 4-5 hours.
    2. \
  2. Drain the soaked rice. In a blender, combine the drained rice, grated coconut (if using), and a little water. Grind to a very smooth batter.
    3. \
  3. Transfer the batter to a large bowl. Add salt and gradually add the remaining water until the batter is very thin and watery, like buttermilk. This is crucial for "neer" (water) dosa.
    4. \
  4. Heat a non-stick tawa or cast-iron griddle over medium-high heat. Lightly grease it with oil.
    5. \
  5. Pour a ladleful of the very thin batter onto the hot tawa, starting from the edges and moving towards the center, filling any gaps. The batter should spread easily and form a lacy pattern.
    6. \
  6. Drizzle a little oil around the edges. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the edges start to lift and the dosa looks cooked and translucent. Neer dosas are typically cooked only on one side.
    7. \
  7. Gently fold the dosa in half or quarters and remove from the tawa. Repeat for remaining batter.
    8. \
\

Tips or Variations: The batter must be very thin for authentic neer dosa. Do not ferment the batter. Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or a variety of curries like chicken curry or fish curry. It's a great gluten-free option.

\

Personal Note: These incredibly light and soft dosas are a staple from Karnataka, perfect for a simple and healthy meal!

Dal Tadka (Spiced Yellow Lentil Soup)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 30 mins

\

Serves: 4

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Toor Dal (split pigeon peas)
    • \
  • 3 cups water
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • For Tadka:
    • \
  • 2 tbsp Ghee or Oil
    • \
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)
    • \
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
    • \
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 large tomato, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
    • \
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Wash dal thoroughly and pressure cook with water, turmeric, and a pinch of salt for 3-4 whistles until soft. Mash lightly.
    2. \
  2. For tadka, heat ghee/oil in a separate pan. Add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add curry leaves and green chilies.
    3. \
  3. Add chopped onions and saut\'e9 until translucent.
    4. \
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut\'e9 for a minute until raw smell disappears.
    5. \
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
    6. \
  6. Stir in red chili powder and garam masala. Cook for a minute.
    7. \
  7. Pour the cooked dal into the tadka. Mix well and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
    8. \
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with rice or roti.
    9. \
\

Tips or Variations: For a smoky flavor, give a charcoal dhungar (smoking) to the dal. Add a few drops of lemon juice before serving.

\

Personal Note: A comforting meal that always reminds me of home. Simple yet incredibly satisfying.

Lady Fingers Bhaji (Okra Stir-fry)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 25 mins

\

Serves: 2-3

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 250g Lady Fingers (Okra), washed, thoroughly dried, and sliced into 1/2 inch pieces
    • \
  • 2 tbsp Oil
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin seeds (Jeera)
    • \
  • Pinch of Asafoetida (Hing)
    • \
  • 1 medium Onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1-2 Green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
    • \
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Amchur (dry mango powder) or juice of 1/2 lemon
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Ensure lady fingers are completely dry before slicing to avoid sliminess.
    2. \
  2. Heat oil in a wide non-stick pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add asafoetida.
    3. \
  3. Add chopped onion and green chilies. Saut\'e9 until the onion turns translucent.
    4. \
  4. Add the sliced lady fingers to the pan. Mix well.
    5. \
  5. Cook on medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sliminess reduces and okra starts to turn tender-crisp.
    6. \
  6. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the okra.
    7. \
  7. Continue to cook on medium-low heat, stirring every few minutes, until the lady fingers are fully cooked, tender, and slightly crisp (about 10-15 more minutes). Avoid covering the pan, as this can make them slimy.
    8. \
  8. Once cooked, turn off the heat. Add amchur powder or squeeze fresh lemon juice and mix gently.
    9. \
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with roti, paratha, or dal-rice.
    10. \
\

Tips or Variations: Drying okra thoroughly is crucial to prevent sliminess. You can also add a tablespoon of besan (gram flour) while cooking to absorb moisture and make it crispier. For a tangy twist, add a small tomato along with onions.

\

Personal Note: A simple yet flavorful side dish that's a staple in many Indian homes. My favorite way to enjoy okra!

Pitla (Maharashtrian Gram Flour Curry)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 25 mins

\

Serves: 3-4

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 1 cup Besan (gram flour)
    • \
  • 4 cups Water (adjust for desired consistency)
    • \
  • 2 tbsp Oil
    • \
  • 1 tsp Cumin seeds (Jeera)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Mustard seeds (Rai)
    • \
  • Pinch of Asafoetida (Hing)
    • \
  • 1 medium Onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic-Green Chili paste (or 1 inch ginger, 4-5 cloves garlic, 2-3 green chilies crushed)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
    • \
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
    • \
  • Lemon juice (optional, for serving)
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. In a large bowl, combine besan with 1 cup of water. Whisk well to form a smooth, lump-free batter. Set aside.
    2. \
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add asafoetida.
    3. \
  3. Add finely chopped onion and ginger-garlic-green chili paste. Saut\'e9 until the onion turns translucent and the raw smell of ginger-garlic disappears (about 3-5 minutes).
    4. \
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Saut\'e9 for 1 minute until fragrant.
    5. \
  5. Reduce the heat to low. Gradually pour the besan batter into the pan while continuously stirring to prevent lumps.
    6. \
  6. Keep stirring as the mixture begins to thicken. Slowly add the remaining 3 cups of water (or more/less depending on desired consistency), stirring continuously to ensure no lumps form.
    7. \
  7. Add salt to taste. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low.
    8. \
  8. Cover the pan and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the besan is thoroughly cooked and the pitla reaches a thick, pasty consistency. It should not taste raw.
    9. \
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with jowar roti, bajra roti, or steamed rice, and a squeeze of lemon juice if desired.
    10. \
\

Tips or Variations: For a thinner consistency (like a soup), add more water. For a dry version (Zunka), use less water. You can add a pinch of sugar to balance the flavors. Some variations include adding a dollop of yogurt to the batter before cooking for a slightly tangy taste.

\

Personal Note: A wholesome and quick meal, perfect for days when you're out of fresh vegetables or want something simple yet satisfying!

Soft Chapatis (Indian Flatbread)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 30 mins

\

Serves: 4-5 (makes 10-12 chapatis)

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients:
\
    \
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp salt
    • \
  • 1 tsp oil (optional)
    • \
  • Approx. 1 cup warm water (as needed)
    • \
  • Extra flour for dusting
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. In a large bowl, combine whole wheat flour and salt.
    2. \
  2. Gradually add warm water, kneading continuously, until a soft, pliable dough forms. Add oil if using, and knead for another 2-3 minutes until smooth.
    3. \
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
    4. \
  4. Divide the dough into equal small balls (lemon-sized).
    5. \
  5. Dust a rolling surface with flour. Take one dough ball, flatten it, and roll it into a thin circle (about 6-7 inches diameter).
    6. \
  6. Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium-high heat. Place the rolled chapati on it.
    7. \
  7. When small bubbles appear on the surface (about 30 seconds), flip it.
    8. \
  8. Cook the other side for another 30-45 seconds. Then, either directly place it on a flame (if using gas stove) or press gently with a cloth on the tawa until it puffs up.
    9. \
  9. Remove from heat, apply a little ghee or butter (optional). Stack in a casserole to keep them soft.
    10. \
\

Tips or Variations: The key to soft chapatis is a soft dough and proper resting time. You can add a pinch of sugar to the dough for a slightly sweeter taste.

\

Personal Note: Nothing beats fresh, hot chapatis straight off the tawa!

Aloo Paratha (Potato Stuffed Flatbread)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 45 mins

\

Serves: 3-4 (makes 6-8 parathas)

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients for Dough:
\
    \
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp salt
    • \
  • 1 tsp oil (optional)
    • \
  • Approx. 3/4 cup water (as needed)
    • \
\
Ingredients for Filling:
\
    \
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
    • \
  • 1/2 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
    • \
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
    • \
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh coriander
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp roasted cumin powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala (optional)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. Prepare Dough: In a bowl, combine whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and oil (if using), kneading to form a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let rest for 15-20 minutes.
    2. \
  2. Prepare Filling: In a separate bowl, combine mashed potatoes, chopped onion, green chilies, coriander, red chili powder, roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, and salt. Mix well to form a uniform filling.
    3. \
  3. Divide the dough into equal lemon-sized balls. Divide the potato filling into equal portions, slightly smaller than the dough balls.
    4. \
  4. Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your fingers. Place one portion of the potato filling in the center.
    5. \
  5. Bring the edges of the dough together to enclose the filling, sealing it completely. Gently flatten the stuffed ball.
    6. \
  6. Dust the stuffed ball with flour and gently roll it into a round paratha (about 6-7 inches diameter) using a rolling pin. Be careful not to let the filling come out.
    7. \
  7. Heat a tawa (griddle) on medium-high heat. Place the rolled paratha on it.
    8. \
  8. Cook for about 1 minute until small bubbles appear. Flip the paratha.
    9. \
  9. Apply a little oil or ghee on the top side. Flip again and apply oil/ghee on the other side.
    10. \
  10. Press gently with a spatula, cooking until golden brown spots appear on both sides and the paratha is cooked through. It should puff up slightly.
    11. \
  11. Remove from tawa and serve hot. Repeat for remaining parathas.
    12. \
\

Tips or Variations: Ensure the potato filling is cool before stuffing to prevent the dough from tearing. You can add grated paneer or finely chopped fenugreek leaves to the filling for variations. Serve with yogurt, pickle, or butter.

\

Personal Note: A hearty and flavorful breakfast or lunch, perfect for a comforting meal!

    2. \
  2. Add 2 tbsp oil or ghee. Gradually add warm water, kneading to form a soft, smooth, and pliable dough. Ensure the dough is not too sticky or too stiff. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
    3. \
  3. Divide the dough into equal lemon-sized balls.
    4. \

    9. \
  9. Remove from tawa and serve hot. Repeat for remaining parathas.
    10. \
\

Tips or Variations: To reduce bitterness from methi, sprinkle salt on chopped leaves, let sit for 10 minutes, then squeeze out excess water before adding to dough. You can add a pinch of sugar to balance the taste. Serve with curd, pickle, or chutney.

Personal Note: A wholesome and aromatic paratha, perfect for a healthy breakfast or light meal, especially during winters!

  • 1 large Onion, thinly sliced

  • 1 medium Potato, thinly sliced or julienned
    • \
  • 1/2 cup Spinach leaves, chopped (optional)

  • 1-2 Green chilies, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste (optional)
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder

    • \

    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin powder
    • \
  • 1/2 tsp Carom seeds (Ajwain), crushed
    • \
  • Pinch of Asafoetida (Hing)
    • \
  • Salt to taste
    • \
  • 1/2 cup Water (or as needed for batter)
    • \
  • Oil for deep frying
    • \
\
Method:
\
    \
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine besan, rice flour (if using), turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, carom seeds, asafoetida, and salt. Mix well.
    2. \
  2. Add the sliced onion, potato, spinach (if using), green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste (if using) to the dry ingredients.
    3. \
  3. Gradually add water, mixing with your hands, until all the vegetables are coated with a thick batter. The batter should be thick enough to cling to the vegetables, not runny. Do not add too much water initially; the vegetables will release some moisture.
    4. \
  4. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadhai or deep pan over medium-high heat.
    5. \
  5. Once the oil is hot, drop small portions of the pakoda mixture into the oil using your hands or a spoon. Do not overcrowd the pan.
    6. \
  6. Fry in batches, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy on all sides.
    7. \
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
    8. \
  8. Serve hot with green chutney, tomato ketchup, or a hot cup of chai.
    9. \
\

Tips or Variations: The key to crispy pakodas is a thick batter and hot oil. You can use a variety of vegetables like cauliflower, paneer, or even bread slices. Add a pinch of baking soda to the batter for extra crispiness (optional).

Chole Bhature (Spiced Chickpea Curry with Fried Bread)

\
\
\

Prep & Cook Time: 60 mins (plus overnight soaking for chole)

\

Serves: 4

\
\
\ \
\
\
Ingredients for Chole (Chickpea Curry):
\
    \
  • 1 cup Kabuli Chana (Chickpeas), soaked overnight
    • \
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped
    • \
  • 1 large Tomato, pureed

    • \

\
Ingredients for Bhature:
\
    \
  • 2 cups Maida (all-purpose flour)
    • \
  • 1/4 cup Semolina (Sooji/Rava)
    • \

    • \

    • \

    • \

    • \

    • \
  • 2 tbsp Oil or Ghee, plus more for deep frying
    • \

    • \
\
Method for Chole:
\
    \
  1. Drain soaked chickpeas. Pressure cook with enough water (approx. 3 cups), salt, and the tea bag/leaves (if using) for 6-7 whistles until very soft. Discard tea bag.
    2. \
  2. Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onion and saut\'e9 until golden brown.
    3. \
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Saut\'e9 for a minute.
    4. \
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
    5. \
  5. Add turmeric, chole masala, red chili, and coriander powders. Saut\'e9 for 2 minutes.
    6. \
  6. Add the cooked chickpeas (along with their cooking water) to the masala. Add amchur powder and adjust salt.
    7. \
  7. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, mashing some chickpeas for thickness. Garnish with fresh coriander.
    8. \
\
Method for Bhature:
\
    \
  1. In a large bowl, combine maida, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
    2. \
  2. Add yogurt and 2 tbsp oil/ghee. Mix well.
    3. \
  3. Gradually add warm water and knead into a soft, smooth, and elastic dough. Knead for 8-10 minutes until very pliable.
    4. \
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest in a warm place for 2-3 hours (or until doubled in size).
    5. \
  5. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadhai or deep pan over medium-high heat.
    6. \
  6. Take a small portion of the dough and roll it into an oval or round shape (about 6-7 inches). Do not roll too thin.
    7. \
  7. Gently slide the rolled bhatura into the hot oil. Press gently with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
    8. \
  8. Once puffed, flip and fry until golden brown on both sides.
    9. \
  9. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels. Serve hot immediately.
    10. \
\

Tips or Variations: For chole, adding a tea bag gives a dark color. For bhature, proper kneading and resting are key for soft, fluffy results. Serve with sliced onions, pickle, and a lemon wedge.

Personal Note: A classic North Indian combo that's incredibly satisfying and a true weekend indulgence!
